Four Layers of AI Infrastructure

1. GPU & NVLink

Modern GPUs like A100 and H100 are the foundation for training large AI models. NVLink offers fast interconnect between GPUs, helping reduce bottlenecks in multi-GPU jobs.

2. RDMA Networking

RDMA (Remote Direct Memory Access) allows high-speed, low-latency communication across machines. It's ideal for synchronizing parameters in distributed deep learning setups.

3. Kubernetes

Kubernetes provides scalable and automated orchestration for AI workloads. It’s useful for managing resources, scheduling jobs, and running experiments reliably.

4. AI-Native Apps

From training pipelines to inference APIs, the application layer ties everything together and brings your models into production or interactive use cases.
