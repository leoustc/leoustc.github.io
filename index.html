Four Layers of AI Infrastructure

1. GPU & NVLink

GPUs are the workhorses of AI workloads. Modern setups like the NVIDIA A100/H100 deliver huge memory bandwidth and NVLink for fast GPU-to-GPU communication — very helpful for large model training and inference.

2. RDMA Networking

RDMA makes node-to-node communication fast and efficient, which is critical when training models across multiple machines. It minimizes CPU overhead and helps with synchronization during distributed training.

3. Kubernetes for Orchestration

Kubernetes helps manage containerized AI workloads in a scalable way. Whether you're running a single-node experiment or a multi-node cluster, it makes things more repeatable and production-friendly.

4. AI-Native Apps

These include training pipelines, inference APIs, and data preparation workflows. A solid app layer helps bring everything together and gets your models into real-world use.
